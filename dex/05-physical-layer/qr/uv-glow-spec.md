# UV / Glow-in-the-Dark QR Sticker Spec

**Covert, high-prestige dead-drop variant.**

---

## Concept

UV-reactive and glow-in-the-dark QR stickers add a **stealth layer** to the dead-drop system.

**Core mechanic:**  
1. Standard QR codes are visible in daylight  
2. **UV QR codes** only appear under blacklight (365nm–395nm)  
3. **Glow-in-the-dark QR codes** charge under light, visible in darkness  
4. Creates **discovery gameplay** — operators must know where to look and bring tools  

---

## Why This Matters

### Aesthetic Alignment

**Encrypted cabal, not public bulletin board.**

UV/glow QR codes fit the Cockroach Protocol identity:

- **Covert operations** — not everyone can see these drops  
- **Tool requirement** — need UV flashlight or knowledge of glow behavior  
- **Prestige signaling** — finding UV drops = higher operator skill  
- **Tactical elegance** — hidden in plain sight, revealed by preparation  

### Gameplay Benefits

1. **Higher difficulty tier** — UV drops are automatically Hard+ difficulty  
2. **Reduced vandalism risk** — invisible to non-operators  
3. **Location specificity** — can place in semi-public spaces without visual pollution  
4. **Ceremonial unlock** — discovering a UV drop feels like espionage  

---

## Material Options

### UV-Reactive Ink

**Invisible under normal light, glows under UV blacklight.**

**Materials:**  
- UV-reactive vinyl stickers  
- UV ink on transparent substrate  
- UV-reactive paint (for DIY application)  

**Wavelength:** 365nm or 395nm UV flashlight (portable, $10–$30)  

**Colors:**  
- Blue (most common, bright)  
- Green (high visibility)  
- Red (lower visibility, more subtle)  
- Yellow (moderate visibility)  

**Recommendation:** Blue or green UV ink for maximum scan reliability.  

---

### Glow-in-the-Dark (Phosphorescent)

**Charges under light, glows in darkness (no UV required).**

**Materials:**  
- Phosphorescent vinyl stickers  
- Glow powder mixed with clear coat (DIY)  
- Pre-printed glow QR stickers  

**Charge source:**  
- Sunlight (best)  
- Bright indoor light (slower charge)  
- UV flashlight (fastest charge)  

**Glow duration:**  
- High-quality phosphorescent: 8–12 hours visible glow  
- Standard phosphorescent: 2–4 hours visible glow  

**Colors:**  
- Green (brightest, longest glow)  
- Blue (moderate glow)  
- Yellow (dim glow)  

**Recommendation:** Green phosphorescent for best scan reliability in low light.  

---

## Hybrid Option: UV + Glow

**Combine both materials for maximum stealth and flexibility.**

**Structure:**  
1. **Base layer:** Standard black QR on white vinyl (visible backup)  
2. **Top layer:** UV-reactive or phosphorescent ink overlay  

**Benefits:**  
- Fallback scanning if UV/glow fails  
- Standard users can still scan if they find it  
- Operators with UV tools get prestige confirmation  

---

## Placement Strategy

### UV-Only Drops (Invisible Mode)

**Best for:**  
- Restricted-access locations (embassy interiors, government offices)  
- Semi-public spaces where visible stickers would be removed  
- High-prestige objectives (Bureaucracy Hard drops)  

**Placement tips:**  
- Near existing signage or frames (blend in)  
- On dark surfaces (UV sticker invisible until scanned)  
- Indoor locations (protected from weather)  

**Operator requirement:**  
- Must carry UV flashlight (365nm or 395nm)  
- Must know approximate location (hints in objectives)  

---

### Glow-in-the-Dark Drops (Low-Light Mode)

**Best for:**  
- Nighttime city runs  
- Indoor locations with low ambient light  
- Gear domain objectives (operators check gear at night)  

**Placement tips:**  
- Charge sticker during day (place in sunlit areas)  
- Indoor placements near windows or bright lights  
- Outdoor placements in shaded areas (protected from direct sun fade)  

**Operator requirement:**  
- Must visit location at night or in low light  
- Optional: Bring UV flashlight to charge sticker on-site  

---

### Hybrid Drops (Standard + UV/Glow)

**Best for:**  
- Medium difficulty drops (accessible but prestigious)  
- Testing UV/glow viability before full rollout  
- Locations where removal risk is moderate  

**Placement tips:**  
- Standard QR visible, UV/glow layer adds prestige  
- Operators without UV tools can still scan  
- UV/glow layer serves as "operator verified" proof

## Technical Considerations

### QR Code Scannability

**Challenge:** UV and glow inks may reduce scan reliability.

**Solutions:**  
1. **Increase QR module size** — Use 50mm or 75mm stickers (not 30mm)  
2. **High contrast** — Dark UV ink on light substrate (or vice versa)  
3. **Error correction** — Use QR Level H (30% error correction)  
4. **Test before deployment** — Print test batch, scan under UV/low light  

---

### Weather Resistance

**UV stickers:**  
- Indoor placement preferred (UV ink can fade faster outdoors)  
- Use UV-resistant laminate for outdoor placements  

**Glow stickers:**  
- Phosphorescent material degrades under direct sunlight over time  
- Indoor or shaded outdoor placements only  
- Expected lifespan: 6–12 months outdoors, 2+ years indoors  

---

### Security Concerns

**Problem:** UV stickers are less visible, but once discovered, easier to photograph and share.

**Mitigation:**  
1. **GPS verification** — App checks user is at location when scanning  
2. **One-time use** — Each drop scanned once per user  
3. **Verify token in URL** — Prevents screenshot sharing  
4. **Rotate drops** — Replace UV drops quarterly to prevent stale intel

## Pilot Plan

### Phase 1: Testing (2–4 weeks)

1. **Print test batch:**  
   - 5x UV-reactive QR stickers (blue ink, 50mm)  
   - 5x Glow-in-the-dark QR stickers (green, 50mm)  
   - 5x Hybrid (standard + UV overlay)  

2. **Deploy in controlled locations:**  
   - Own apartment (indoor test)  
   - Friend's office (indoor test)  
   - Public library (semi-public test)  

3. **Test variables:**  
   - Scan reliability under UV (365nm vs 395nm)  
   - Glow duration after sunlight charge  
   - Scan reliability in low light  
   - Weather resistance (outdoor placement for 2 weeks)  

---

### Phase 2: Riga Pilot (1–2 months)

1. **Deploy 10–15 UV drops:**  
   - Bureaucracy domain focus (embassies, consulates, gov offices)  
   - Placement inside lobbies or waiting areas (semi-restricted)  

2. **Deploy 5–10 Glow drops:**  
   - Nighttime city run objectives  
   - Gear domain nodes (operators check backup gear at night)  

3. **Track metrics:**  
   - Scan success rate (UV vs glow)  
   - User feedback ("finding UV drops is too hard" vs "love the stealth")  
   - Vandalism/removal rate (compare to standard QR)  

---

### Phase 3: Rollout Decision

**Criteria for full rollout:**  
- UV scan success rate >80% (with UV flashlight)  
- Glow scan success rate >70% (in low light)  
- Positive user feedback on "encrypted cabal" aesthetic  
- Lower removal rate than standard QR stickers  

**If successful:**  
- Standardize UV drops for all Hard+ Bureaucracy objectives  
- Offer glow drops as optional "nighttime operator" challenges  
- Create UV flashlight as recommended gear in Gear domain
